6th Wisconsin Infantry, Company B (1861–1863)

Battle of Gainesville (Brawner’s Farm)

August 28, 1862 | Gainesville, Virginia

The 6th Wisconsin, under Colonel Lysander Cutler, faced Stonewall Jackson’s forces in a fierce skirmish, losing ~133 men. Robert, in Company B, fought in close-quarters combat, helping establish the Iron Brigade’s reputation.

Battle of Second Bull Run

August 28–30, 1862 | Manassas, Virginia

Under General John Pope, the 6th Wisconsin defended against Confederate assaults, losing ~50 men. Robert engaged in defensive volleys, surviving the Union retreat.

Battle of South Mountain

September 14, 1862 | South Mountain, Maryland

The 6th Wisconsin supported the Iron Brigade’s advance at Turner’s Gap, facing light resistance. Robert maneuvered and fired with Company B, preparing for Antietam.

Battle of Antietam

September 17, 1862 | Sharpsburg, Maryland

In the Cornfield, Robert shouted, “Not yet! I have a few more cartridges left!” and fired alone under heavy fire, earning his “Bombastes Furioso” nickname. The regiment lost ~152 men.

Battle of Fredericksburg

December 13, 1862 | Fredericksburg, Virginia

The 6th Wisconsin faced Confederate fire at Marye’s Heights, losing ~30 men. Robert advanced or held lines, surviving the Union defeat.

Battle of Chancellorsville

May 1–6, 1863 | Chancellorsville, Virginia

The 6th Wisconsin held defensive lines, losing ~70 men. Robert fired and skirmished, remaining active despite Confederate victory.

Battle of Gettysburg

July 1–3, 1863 | Gettysburg, Pennsylvania

At McPherson’s Ridge and Railroad Cut, Robert was wounded in the shoulder. A quote attributed to him, “God, you ain’t going back are you? Not yet. I still have a few more cartridges!”, may reflect his defiance (possibly from Antietam). The regiment lost ~152 men.

Battle of Buckland Mills (Capture)

October 19, 1863 | Broad Run, Virginia

Despite an unhealed wound, Robert grabbed a pistol and ran from the commissary to join the 6th Wisconsin’s defense against Confederate cavalry, only to be captured. He died at Andersonville Prison on June 28, 1864.
